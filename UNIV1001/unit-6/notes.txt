Fixed Mindset vs Growth Mindset — Notes

SIMILARITIES:
- Both are cognitive frameworks that shape how people interpret experiences
- Both influence academic performance and motivation
- Both can be present in the same person depending on the situation
- Both are influenced by feedback, environment, and past experiences

FIXED MINDSET:
- Believes intelligence and talent are innate and unchangeable
- Avoids challenges to prevent failure
- Gives up easily when facing obstacles
- Views effort as pointless ("if I were smart, I wouldn't need to try")
- Ignores or rejects constructive criticism
- Feels threatened by the success of others
- Negative bias reinforces this mindset
- Leads to plateauing early and not reaching full potential

GROWTH MINDSET:
- Believes intelligence and abilities can be developed through effort
- Embraces challenges as opportunities to learn
- Persists through setbacks and obstacles
- Views effort as the path to mastery
- Learns from criticism and feedback
- Finds inspiration in the success of others
- Leads to higher achievement over time
- Encourages risk-taking and experimentation in learning

KEY DIFFERENCES:
| Aspect          | Fixed Mindset              | Growth Mindset              |
|-----------------|----------------------------|-----------------------------|
| Intelligence    | Static, born with it       | Developable through effort  |
| Challenges      | Avoided                    | Embraced                    |
| Effort          | Seen as weakness           | Seen as necessary           |
| Feedback        | Rejected/ignored           | Used for improvement        |
| Failure         | Defines identity           | Seen as learning opportunity|
| Others' success | Threatening                | Inspiring                   |

Source: Dweck, C. S. (2006). Mindset: The new psychology of success. Random House.
